About This Project

Multi-Criteria Insurance Recommender System (MCRS)

Most insurance comparison platforms emphasize lowest premium pricing as the dominant ranking criterion. While this appeals to cost-sensitive consumers, it inadvertently fosters adverse selection — users select plans that minimize short-term cost but experience slow claims, poor service quality, and weak renewal experiences later.

This project proposes an AI-driven decision aid that ranks insurance plans by long-term value, integrating signals of cost, coverage quality, and service performance (claims handling, customer satisfaction, and renewal rates).

Methodology

TOPSIS Algorithm

The system uses the Technique for Order of Preference by Similarity to Ideal Solution (TOPSIS), a well-established Multi-Criteria Decision Making (MCDM) method that:

  • Normalizes criteria across different scales
  • Applies user-configurable weights (λ₁, λ₂, λ₃, λ₄)
  • Calculates distances to ideal and anti-ideal solutions
  • Ranks alternatives based on relative closeness

Evaluation Criteria

Cost Efficiency (λ₁)

Normalized premium affordability

Coverage Adequacy (λ₂)

IDV and add-on evaluation

Service Quality (λ₃)

Claims TAT, approval rate, CSAT

Reliability (λ₄)

Renewal rates, complaint ratio
